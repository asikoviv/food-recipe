Chicken Jambalaya

Jambalaya is a rice dish that originated in south Louisiana in the 18th century. While there are countless variations, a standard jambalaya contains rice, protein, seasoning vegetables and spices.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts, chopped.
  • 100 g chicken sausage, sliced.
  • 1 onion, diced.
  • roughly chopped corriander leaves.
  • 2 tbsp olive oil.
  • 2 tsp garlic, crushed.
  • 1 tbsp black pepper.
  • 1 red and green bell pepper, sliced.
  • 1 cup long-grain rice.
  • 400 g tomato paste.
  • 1 Tbsp Cajun spice.
  • 1 Tbsp paprika.
  • 500 ml chicken stock.

Preparation Steps

  1. Start by chopping off the green and red bell paper and dice onions.
  2. Marinate boneless skinless chicken breasts with salt,cajun spice and paprika.
  3. In a clean pan, add olive oil followed with sliced chicken sausages fry until golden brown on each side take it of the pan and cook the chicken breast.
  4. Remove them from the pan then add chopped bell papers,crushed garlic and diced onions once it softens and tomato paste and rice.
  5. Add chicken stock followed with chicken breasts and sausages then add black pepper.
  6. Cover the pan and let it cook under low heat for about 12 mins.
  7. Garnish with corriander leaves serve and enjoy.
